# NDA Compliance

## Important: Protect Your Confidentiality Agreement

As you work through this module on your Real Life Project, you will be implementing continuous delivery practices, pipeline architecture, and system testing frameworks. While you do this work, **please respect your NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)** with your organization.

---

## What You Can Share

You are welcome to discuss and reference technical implementation details that you would normally discuss in professional settings like interviews, conference talks, or meetups:

**Technology Stack:**

- Tooling: "We use JIRA for issue tracking," "We use GitLab for our CI/CD pipeline," "We use Docker Hub for artifact storage"
- Infrastructure: "We deploy to AWS," "We use Kubernetes," "We use PostgreSQL for our database"
- Architecture: "We have a microservices architecture," "We use a monolith," "We have frontend and backend services"

**Process & Practice:**

- "We implemented a four-stage pipeline: Commit, Acceptance, QA, Production"
- "We automated our Commit Stage to run on every push"
- "We set up system tests in an Acceptance Stage"
- "We use semantic versioning for artifacts"
- "We consolidated deployment scripts across environments"

**General Challenges & Solutions:**

- "We faced challenges integrating E2E tests into the pipeline"
- "We had to refactor our artifact versioning"
- "We struggled with test reliability and stability"

---

## What You Should NOT Share

Do not reveal anything that relates to your organization's business, domain, customers, or confidential systems:

**Do Not Share:**

- Business logic or domain-specific details: "Our system handles X type of transactions," "We process Y kind of data"
- Customer information: Names, counts, geographic locations, or any identifying details
- Financial data: Revenue, pricing, costs, or business metrics
- Proprietary algorithms or business rules
- Customer names or list of clients
- Internal organizational structure or specific team names (use "my team" or "engineering team")
- Product roadmap or unreleased features
- Specific performance metrics or system scale that could reveal business information

---

## The Interview Test

A good rule of thumb: **Speak at the level you would speak in a job interview or external tech meetup.**

In those settings, you discuss technology, architecture, challenges you solved, and practices you implemented. You don't discuss your company's business model, customer details, or confidential strategies.

Apply the same standard here.

---

## Examples

**✓ Appropriate to Share:**

- "We set up a GitLab CI/CD pipeline with four stages"
- "We automated artifact versioning using Docker tags"
- "We struggled with E2E test flakiness and solved it by X"
- "We use JIRA custom fields for bug type tracking"
- "Our acceptance stage deploys to a staging environment"

**✗ Do Not Share:**

- "Our system processes customer payment data for X clients"
- "We handle Y million transactions per day"
- "Our primary customer is [Company Name]"
- "Our proprietary algorithm does X"
- "Our revenue depends on Z"

---

## If You're Unsure

When in doubt, ask yourself:

1. **Would I say this in a job interview?** If yes, probably okay.
2. **Does this reveal something about our business or customers?** If yes, don't include it.
3. **Is this technical implementation vs. business domain?** Stick to technical; avoid domain.
4. **Could this information harm the company competitively?** If yes, don't share.

---

## Questions or Concerns?

If you're unsure whether something violates your NDA, **err on the side of caution and don't include it.** You can always describe your implementation more generically.

For example, instead of "We process X type of transactions," say "We built a system that validates data and processes requests through multiple stages."

**Respect your organization's trust. Protect confidential information. Share technical learning.**
